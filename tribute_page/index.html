Eleanor Roosevelt

"In the long run, we shape our lives, and we shape ourselves. The process never ends until we die. And the choices we make are ultimately our own responsibility." Eleanor Roosevelt

Eleanor Roosevelt Life Timeline:

  • 1884
    • Born in New York City, October 11th, to Elliott and Anna Hall Roosevelt
  • 1905-1920
    • Marries Franklin D. Roosevelt in New York City, March 17th
    • Moves family to Albany after FDR elected to state senate.
    • Later calls this time "the beginning of my independence"
    • Works with the D.C. Red Cross, the Navy Department, and Navy League to help WWI servicemen
    • Accompanies FDR on tour of European naval installations and battlefields; attends Paris Peace Conference
    • Learns to drive
  • 1920-1932
    • Joins the Women's Trade Union League and works closely with WTUL activists Rose Schneiderman and Maud Swartz
    • Joins the Women's Division of the Democratic State Committee and begins association with Democratic activists Marion Dickerman and Nancy Cook
    • Organizes Democratic women across New York state
    • Lobbies for equal pay for women and child labor amendment
    • Acts as WTUL surrogate in Albany on legislation supporting five day work week
    • FDR wins presidential election in 1932
    • States she will not be a symbol of elegance but rather "plain, ordinary Mrs. Roosevelt"
  • 1933-1939
    • Becomes First Lady and struggles to find an acceptable role in the administration
    • Holds regular press conferences, the first first lady to do so
    • Travels 40,000 miles investigating social and economic conditions
    • Receives and answers 300,000 letters from August 31st - December 31st
    • Closely monitors National Recovery Administration and Federal Emergency Relief Administration projects
    • Resigns from DAR in support of Marian Anderson and helps to arrange Anderson's Lincoln Memorial concert
    • Assists Karl Frank aid him in anti Hitler underground
    • Helps European labor and socialist deputies (and some of their families) relocate to America
    • Named one of "The Ten Most Powerful People in Washington"
    • Sixty-seven percent of Americans approve of her conduct
  • 1940-1945
    • After meeting with civil rights groups, urges FDR to establish Fair Employment Practices Commission (FEPC)
    • Addresses nation on radio the day Pearl Harbor is bombed
    • Works to prevent internment of Japanese-Americans
    • Begins "If You Ask Me," a monthly question and answer column which runs until November 1962
    • Urges women's employment in war industries
    • Offers critical support to establishment of War Refugee Board
    • FDR dies, April 12, 1945 in Warm Springs, Georgia
    • Tells the press "the story is over"
    • Joins NAACP board of directors and becomes lifetime member of National Council of Negro Women
    • Declines offers to run for Governor of New York, Senator from New York, and Director of National Citizens Political Action Committee
    • Truman signs UN Charter and appoints ER to US delegation, where she is quickly dubbed "the hardest working delegate"
  • 1962
    • Dies from complications stemming from tuberculosis, November 7 1962
